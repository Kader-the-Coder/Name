Zayd

tap the name

Increase • Growth • Abundance

From the Arabic root ز-ي-د meaning “to increase”

Zayd ibn Thabit (رضي الله عنه) was a companion of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, known for his intelligence and literacy.

He recorded the Qur’an as it was revealed and later led its compilation into a single manuscript.

His legacy is one of precision, trust, and preservation.

بَارَكَ اللَّهُ لَكَ فِي الْمَوْهُوبِ لَكَ، وَشَكَرْتَ الْوَاهِبَ، وَبَلَغَ أَشُدَّهُ، وَرُزِقْتَ بِرَّهُ

“May Allah bless you in what has been given to you, may you thank the Giver, may he reach maturity, and be granted righteousness.”

أُعِيذُكَ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّةِ مِنْ كُلِّ شَيْطَانٍ وَهَامَّةٍ وَمِنْ كُلِّ عَيْنٍ لَامَّةٍ

“I seek protection for you in the perfect words of Allah from every devil, harmful creature, and evil eye.”
